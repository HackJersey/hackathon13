2005

After representing New Jersey in the U.S. Senate for five years, Jon Corzine handily defeated former West Windsor Mayor Doug Forrester in the 2005 gubernatorial race by nearly five percentage points. Despite former Democratic Gov. James McGreevy's rocky departure in 2004, Corzine, who spent at least $43 million of his own fortune, triumphed in what was marked as a bitter campaign between the two candidates.

2009

The 2009 contest between Corzine and U.S. Attorney Chris Christie saw the Garden State elect the first Republican to statewide office in 12 years. In defeating Corzine and independent challenger Christopher Daggett, Christie ran on a platform of reducing property taxes and capitalized on the state’s sluggish economy en route to gathering nearly 49 percent of the vote.
